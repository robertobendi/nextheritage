# DIRECTIONS

## Candidate A — Italian poster × motorsport heritage

**One-line signature**: A pomodoro-red top band with the crest centered like a rally start banner, then the event name set in 9rem condensed Anton caps bleeding off the right edge over a cream field ruled with thin brass pinstripes and corner ornaments — reads instantly as a 1960s Ticino racing poster.

**Palette**:
- `#f4efe2` — Crema — surface
- `#1a1410` — Nero Pista — ink
- `#c1272d` — Pomodoro Rosso — accent 1 / primary CTA
- `#d4a017` — Ottone — accent 2 / rules + ornaments
- `#1f6412` — Pistacchio Ticino — highlight / secondary tag
- `#7a1e22` — Bordò — deep frame borders
- `#ffffff` — Bianco Croce — Swiss cross + flag squares

**Typography**:
- Display: Anton at weight 400 (condensed poster caps, set huge)
- Body: Spectral at weight 400 / 500
- Accent / eyebrow: Archivo at weight 700 (tracked-out small caps)

**Hero composition**: Full-width hero on cream. A saturated pomodoro-red banner spans the top edge; the refined crest sits dead-center on it like a finish-line emblem. Below, the headline ("GOTTARDO CLASSIC — 21 GIUGNO") in oversized Anton caps bleeds off the right margin, with a brass pinstripe rule and a date stamp underneath. One candid event photo sits framed in a thin black-and-gold double border to the lower left. A single red "DIVENTA SOCIO" pill is the only filled button.

**Decorative kit**:
- Brass pinstripe double-rules — around hero frame and every card
- Checkered-flag corner ornaments — top corners of hero + footer band
- Eyebrow labels in tracked Archivo caps — above every H2
- Swiss white-cross chip — beside the federation/credibility line
- Numbered route markers (●1 ●2 ●3) — on the event itinerary

**Voice angle**: Confident poster copy — short declarative event lines, dates in display caps, warmth carried by one-sentence pull quotes rather than long lyrical paragraphs.

**Why this fits**: Ticino is Italian-speaking and the brand IS motorsport — the period-poster idiom makes the racing red/gold/black logo feel native instead of bolted on, and saturated brights plus the candid photos read young while the heraldic crest and Swiss cross carry heritage credibility. Directly serves the primary objective: dated events headlined big, one dominant join CTA.

## Candidate B — Mid-century print (Paul Rand / Saul Bass)

**One-line signature**: Muted inks on kraft paper, a big flat geometric car silhouette built from 3 brick-red and mustard shapes anchoring an asymmetric grid, with generous negative space and a single Syne-set headline stacked top-left — calm, designed, archival, nothing like a poster shout.

**Palette**:
- `#e7e0cf` — Carta Avana — surface
- `#1a1410` — Inchiostro — ink
- `#c1272d` — Rosso Mattone — accent 1
- `#b8902a` — Senape Ossidata — accent 2 (muted gold)
- `#2e5d4f` — Verde Petrolio — accent 3
- `#d8c3a0` — Sabbia — secondary panels
- `#ffffff` — Bianco Croce — Swiss cross cue

**Typography**:
- Display: Syne at weight 800 (geometric, retro-futurist)
- Body: Newsreader at weight 400
- Accent / eyebrow: Space Mono at weight 400 (catalog-label feel)

**Hero composition**: Asymmetric 12-column grid on kraft paper. Top-left: eyebrow in Space Mono ("ASSOCIAZIONE · TICINO"), then a two-line Syne headline. Right two-thirds: one large flat geometric illustration — a classic coupé reduced to overlapping brick-red, mustard and petrol shapes (Saul Bass style), no photo in the hero. The crest sits small and monochrome in the corner as a printed seal. Thin mono caption runs along the baseline like a print colophon.

**Decorative kit**:
- Flat geometric car/steering-wheel cutouts — hero + section dividers
- Mono catalog labels (No. 01 / No. 02) — on each event block
- Thin single hairline rules — separating grid sections
- Swiss-cross seal — in the footer colophon
- Paper grain texture — subtle, across all surfaces

**Voice angle**: Spare editorial captions — museum-catalog tone, each event a titled plate with date, route, and one factual line; warmth comes from precision, not adjectives.

**Why this fits**: Mid-century print is the visual language of the very era these classic cars come from, so it reads heritage without nostalgia kitsch — and its restraint plus geometric wit feels intentional and modern, satisfying the generational-bridge tension. The muted palette still carries the brand red and gold, and the calm grid handles long IT/DE strings and four languages gracefully.

## Candidate C — Editorial collage × Risograph zine

**One-line signature**: A scrappy two-spot-ink scrapbook — halftone-dotted candid event photos taped at angles into a 3-photo collage hero, with a 7rem Fraunces serif headline overlapping a clashing Archivo Expanded sub-band, registration offset on the red so the cyan peeks out: a youth-club fanzine, not a corporate site.

**Palette**:
- `#f4efe2` — Newsprint Cream — surface
- `#0e000c` — Carbone — ink
- `#c1272d` — Riso Rosso — accent 1 (spot ink)
- `#d4a017` — Foglia Oro — accent 2
- `#4ebdf5` — Ciano Lago — highlight (second riso spot ink)
- `#370031` — Melanzana — decorative (the legacy plum, repurposed deliberately)
- `#ffffff` — Bianco — paper / cross

**Typography**:
- Display: Fraunces at weight 900 (oversized, occasional italic)
- Body: DM Sans at weight 400
- Accent / eyebrow: Caveat at weight 600 (handwritten marginalia + pull-quotes)

**Hero composition**: Collage hero on cream newsprint. Three candid event snapshots (the real documentary photos) printed in red/cyan halftone, taped at slight angles with washi-tape corners and one polaroid-style white border. A huge Fraunces serif headline ("Born 2 Late 4 Classic") sits half-over the photos; a clashing Archivo Expanded band underneath carries the next date. The crest is rendered as a die-cut sticker peeling at one corner. Handwritten Caveat scribbles annotate the photos ("Gottardo '25 →").

**Decorative kit**:
- Halftone dot screens — on every photo
- Washi tape + peeling-sticker corners — hero photos and crest
- Caveat marginalia + circled annotations — beside photos and quotes
- Registration-offset misprint edge — on red headline bands
- Cyan starbursts / hand-drawn arrows — pointing to CTAs

**Voice angle**: Breezy first-person zine prose with handwritten marginalia pull-quotes — insider, peer-to-peer ("vieni a guidare con noi"), dates and CTAs called out in scribbled annotations.

**Why this fits**: This is the most youth-forward take — it leverages the candid amateur snapshots (currently a weakness) as an authentic strength, speaking to late-teen-to-30s enthusiasts as insiders per the primary objective. Riso red and cyan still seat the brand red and gold, the legacy plum is recovered as a deliberate accent, and the crest-as-sticker keeps heritage present while the whole thing feels handmade and alive.

## Self-check
The three are visibly different in a single screenshot: A is a loud, saturated, ornament-framed Italian race poster (cream + pomodoro, condensed caps bleeding off-edge); B is a calm, muted, flat-geometric mid-century print on kraft paper with deep negative space and no hero photo; C is a messy, taped, halftone two-spot-ink fanzine collage built from candid photos. Different families, different palettes (saturated brights vs. muted kraft inks vs. riso spot inks), different photo treatments (framed vs. none vs. halftone collage), different type spines (Anton vs. Syne vs. Fraunces). All three keep the heraldic crest and Swiss-cross cue and carry the brand red/gold/black/cream as structural accents, not noise. No two landed in the same family — no rewrite needed.
